💰 Financial Agent: analysis, reconciliation and compliance

The financial agent is the vertical withhighest documented ROI— high-volume repetitive processes with structured data. Risk analysis, accounting reconciliation, regulatory reporting and fraud detection are use cases that combine high frequency with high operational cost.

🏗️ Financial Agent Architecture

Tools available

  • • Bloomberg/Refinitiv API (market data)
  • • Financial calculator (NPV, IRR, DCF)
  • • Read-only access to the ERP
  • • Alert system (email/Slack)
  • • PDF report generator

Use cases

  • • Credit risk analysis
  • • Automated accounting reconciliation
  • • Regulatory report (BACEN, CVM)
  • • Detection of anomalies in transactions
  • • Cash flow forecasting

Mandatory compliance

  • • SOX: immutable audit trail
  • • LGPD: customer data
  • • BACEN: banking regulations
  • • Segregation of duties
  • • Double approval above limit

📊 Documented ROI in finance

  • •3-day manual reconciliation → agent:4 hours. 90% reduction in accounting closing time.
  • •Fraud detection:97% accuracyvs 78% rules-based approach (Febraban, 2025)
  • •Agent cost: ~$2,000/month. Replaced cost:2 analysts × R$8,000= savings of R$14,000/month net

⚠️SOX compliance in financial agents

SOX requires that every financial decision be traceable. The agent must log: what data was consulted, what calculation was made, what conclusion was generated and who (human) approved it. Immutable audit trail is not optional — it is a legal requirement. Use append-only storage (CloudTrail, Kafka with long retention) for agent logs.

⚖️ Legal Agent: contract review and legal research

The legal agent speeds up analysis processes that consume hundreds of hours per month. The fundamental rule:the agent advises — the lawyer decides. This distinction isn't just ethical — it's legal. The agent does not practice law; it processes information to assist the qualified professional.

📋 Contract review: how the agent works

1

Parsing and structured extraction

PDF reading tool extracts text, identifies sections (parts, object, obligations, penalties, termination) and structure for analysis.

2

Analysis against precedent basis (RAG)

Compares clauses based on the company's previous contracts, identifies unusual, abusive clauses or clauses that deviate from the standard.

3

Structured Risk Report

Generates a report with risk per clause (high/medium/low), negotiation suggestions and a checklist of points that require the lawyer's attention.

Documented impact

  • ✓Contract review: 8h → 20 minutes (agent) + 30 min human review
  • ✓Identification of risk clauses: 94% recall vs. 78% human tired
  • ✓Contract screening for priority review: 10x greater volume

Critical limitations

  • ✗Agent cannot sign, represent or issue legal opinion
  • ✗Strategic negotiation decisions require a lawyer
  • ✗Interpretation of specific law requires full legal context
  • ✗Final responsibility always lies with the OAB professional

💡Taxonomy of risk clauses

Define and document the taxonomy of risk clauses for your context: unilateral termination clauses, limitation of liability clauses, exclusivity clauses, disproportionate penalties, unfavorable forum of election. The agent is only as good as the taxonomy you define for it to identify.

👥 HR Agent: screening, onboarding and organizational climate

HR focuses on highly repetitive processes with high volume: screening hundreds of resumes, onboarding new employees, repetitive benefits FAQ and climate survey analysis.LGPD in candidate datais the most critical compliance — neglecting it creates immediate legal liabilities.

🔄 HR Agent use cases and tools

Screening:

Reads resume via PDF parser, extracts skills, experience and training, compares with job criteria, generates score and summary for recruiter. 200 CVs in 20 minutes.

Onboarding:

Create personalized checklist by position, send documents, schedule team meetings, configure access via IT tickets, monitor the completion of each stage.

HR FAQ:

Answers questions about benefits, vacations, home office policy. Scale to human HR only for complex cases. Available 24/7 via Slack.

Weather Search:

Analyzes open responses with sentiment analysis, identifies recurring themes, generates reports by department, detects negative eNPS alerts.

⚠️LGPD in candidate data: obligations

  • •Collects only data necessary for evaluating the vacancy (minimization)
  • •Candidate must explicitly consent to processing by AI agent
  • •Right to deletion: candidate can request removal of all data
  • •Automated triage decisions must be reviewable (not black box)
  • •Maximum retention of data from unsuccessful candidates: 6 months

💡Bias in automated screening

LLMs can reproduce labor market biases. Implement: blind screening (remove name, gender, age before analysis), diversity scoring (monitor distribution of approved candidates by profile), periodic audit of outcomes (candidates approved by the agent vs. hired). Undetected bias is a legal and ethical liability.

📈 Commercial Agent (SDR): qualification and automated outreach

SDR (Sales Development Representative) is one of the roles most replicated by agents — high repetition, clear qualification criteria and objective success metrics. The agent operates 24/7 with consistency that humans cannot maintain across hundreds of simultaneous leads.

🔄 SDR Agent Flow

1 Research:searches lead data (LinkedIn, website, news) and company (size, sector, financing, tech stack)
2 Qualification:applies BANT/MEDDIC criteria, calculates lead score, defines priority
3 Customization:write personalized email with reference to specific data about the lead or company
4 Sequence:follow-up program (day 3, 7, 14) with channel variation (email, LinkedIn)
5 CRM:updates Salesforce/HubSpot with all interactions and status
6 Climbing:lead responds positively → schedules meeting with human AE automatically

💡When to scale to human

Configure automatic escalation to human when: lead is high priority ICP (enterprise >500 func.), lead asks specific technical question, lead mentions a direct competitor, lead demonstrates purchasing urgency. The agent takes care of the top-of-funnel; AE closes strategic deals.

⚙️ Operations Agent: incidents, runbooks and on-call

The operations agent is whatsolves incidents at 3 ambefore waking anyone up. Monitor systems, detect anomalies, execute the first steps of the runbook automatically and only wake up the human on duty when really necessary.

🚨 Agent Incident Response Flow

!

Alert detected (Datadog, PagerDuty)

P99 latency above 5s. Agent receives webhook of the alert and begins investigation.

1

Automated diagnostics

Consult logs, metrics and traces. Identifies affected service, probable cause and estimated impact.

2

Runbook execution (safe steps)

Performs low-risk remediation steps: pod restart, cache clear, scaling up. Document each action.

?

Escalation or resolution

If resolved: post status update on Slack + close the ticket. If it didn't solve the problem: wake up the engineer on duty with a complete diagnosis ready.

📊 Impact on MTTR

  • •Average MTTR (Mean Time to Recover) without agent:45 minutes
  • •MTTR with agent executing first steps:12 minutes(73% reduction)
  • •Incidents resolved 100% by the agent (without human wake-up):35%of the total
  • •Quality of diagnosis when scaling: engineer arrives knowing exactly the problem

🎯 Vertical Selection: Where to Deploy First

The choice of the first vertical defines the success or failure of the agent initiative.Start where ROI is fast and risk is lowIt's strategy, not accident. A successful first agent opens up budget and internal support for the next ones.

📊 Vertical Assessment Framework

Criterion Financial Legal RH Commercial Oops
Repetitive volume High Average High High High
Risk of error High Average Average Low Average
Expected ROI Very High High Average High Average
Facility for POC Difficult Average Easy Easy Average

🔢 The 4 selection criteria

1. Repetitive volume

Processes that happen hundreds of times a month with similar logic. If the process occurs rarely, the ROI does not justify the investment in automation.

2. High current cost

Process with high cost in qualified human time or frequent errors. Where every hour saved has great value.

3. Low risk of error

For the first agent, choose where error is correctable. Avoid processes where an agent error has irreversible or high-impact consequences.

4. Available data

Process with structured and accessible data. An agent without data to work with doesn't work. Evaluate quality and affordability before starting.

💡Recommendation for first agent

For most companies, the ideal first agent is:CV screening (HR) ou lead qualification (Commercial). Both have high volume, low risk of fatal error, measurable ROI in weeks, and typically receptive stakeholders. Show quick results and use to build credibility for more complex verticals.
